# Atlas — Speaker Notes
### Lightning Pitch · 3 Minutes · 10 Slides

---

## Slide 1 — Cover
**Target: 0:00 – 0:05**

> "Good morning / afternoon. My name is [your name], and this is Atlas — Cebu's fitness and sports discovery platform. In the next three minutes, I'll show you the problem we're solving, how we solve it, and why now is the right time."

**Tip:** Say it confidently. Let the slide sit for 2 seconds before moving on. Don't rush the opening.

---

## Slide 2 — The Problem
**Target: 0:05 – 0:35**

> "Let me tell you about Maria. She's a BPO worker in IT Park. After her shift, she wants to play badminton — but she has no idea where the nearest court is, whether it's open, or how crowded it gets at 10 PM. She ends up scrolling Facebook groups for 20 minutes and still isn't sure.
>
> This is the reality for hundreds of thousands of people in Cebu every single day.
>
> There is no dedicated platform for fitness discovery here. Google Maps doesn't show crowd levels or fitness-specific filters. People rely on word of mouth. The result? Wasted trips, missed workouts, and frustration."

**Tip:** Slow down on the Maria story. Make eye contact. Let the problem land before moving to the next slide. The story is your hook — don't rush it.

---

## Slide 3 — The Solution
**Target: 0:35 – 1:05**

> "Atlas solves this. It's an interactive map of over 50 gyms, parks, sports courts, and nutrition stores across Cebu — with real-time crowd levels reported by the community.
>
> You can filter by distance, price, amenities like AC or parking, and rating. And our smart suggestion engine tells you the best time to visit — 'Best time: before 9 AM' — so you never show up to a packed gym again.
>
> The best part? You don't even need an account to browse. Zero friction."

**Tip:** Point at the screenshot if you have it on screen. Say "this is the actual app — not a mockup." That line builds instant credibility.

---

## Slide 4 — Market Opportunity
**Target: 1:05 – 1:25**

> "Now let's talk about the opportunity. Cebu City has 950,000 people. Our target market — health-conscious adults aged 18 to 40 — is around 280,000. The Philippine fitness industry is growing at 8.5% annually, driven by a post-pandemic surge in gym memberships and outdoor activity.
>
> The total addressable market for Cebu fitness and sports alone is 180 million pesos. We're targeting 10% of that by Year 3."

**Tip:** Hit the three big numbers clearly: **950K, 280K, 8.5%.** Don't read every market segment — just the headline stat and the SAM. Keep it moving.

---

## Slide 5 — Value Proposition
**Target: 1:25 – 1:45**

> "Our value proposition is simple: the right place, at the right time, every time.
>
> For users — save time, avoid crowds, discover hidden spots, get a personalized feed. For businesses — free visibility to thousands of active users, community credibility through check-ins, and premium analytics on peak hours.
>
> No other platform in Cebu offers this combination. We're not just a map. We're a fitness intelligence platform."

**Tip:** Emphasize **"no other platform in Cebu"** — pause slightly before and after it. That's the key differentiator. Say it like you mean it.

---

## Slide 6 — Competition + Perceptual Map
**Target: 1:45 – 2:00**

> "Every business has competition. Google Maps is generic and global. Strava is fitness-focused but not local. Facebook groups are local but unstructured and slow.
>
> None of them combine fitness-specific discovery with hyper-local context and crowd intelligence. On the perceptual map, we sit in the bottom-right quadrant — fitness-specific and hyper-local. That's a blue ocean. Nobody is there."

**Tip:** Point at the perceptual map dot. Say **"blue ocean"** — investors know exactly what that means. This slide should feel fast and confident.

---

## Slide 7 — Business Model Canvas
**Target: 2:00 – 2:20**

> "Here's how Atlas creates and captures value. Our key partners are local gyms, sports facilities, supplement brands, and the Cebu City LGU. Our value proposition sits at the center — fitness discovery plus crowd intelligence plus community.
>
> We serve 280,000 target users through our web app and social media channels. Revenue comes from premium business listings at 500 to 2,000 pesos per month, featured placement ads, and affiliate commissions. Our cost structure is lean — under 260,000 pesos per year to operate."

**Tip:** Don't read every cell. Walk through it in one breath: **partners → value → customers → revenue → costs.** 20 seconds max. The judges can read the rest.

---

## Slide 8 — Financials
**Target: 2:20 – 2:35**

> "The model is simple: free for users, revenue from businesses. In Year 1, 80 business listings generate 240,000 pesos. By Year 3, 800 listings and 80,000 users bring in 6.4 million pesos. Our operating costs are lean — about 260,000 pesos per year. We project break-even at Month 14."

**Tip:** Hit **Year 1 and Year 3** numbers clearly and let the gap between them tell the growth story. Don't get into the math — just the headline figures.

---

## Slide 9 — Traction + Roadmap
**Target: 2:35 – 2:50**

> "This is not a concept. Atlas is already built and working. We have 50-plus Cebu locations seeded, a full authentication system, crowd intelligence, community check-ins, and an analytics dashboard — all live today.
>
> We surveyed 30 Cebu gym-goers and 100% confirmed the problem. Local gym owners have already expressed interest in free listings.
>
> Next: business dashboard in Q3, mobile app in Q4, and expansion to Mandaue and Lapu-Lapu in 2026."

**Tip:** Say **"already built"** with full confidence. This is your strongest validation point. The fact that it's a working app — not a mockup — is a major differentiator in a pitch competition.

---

## Slide 10 — The Ask
**Target: 2:50 – 3:00**

> "We're asking for 500,000 pesos in seed funding. This unlocks our mobile app launch, a marketing campaign to reach 25,000 users in Year 1, and a business development push to onboard 200-plus listings.
>
> 950,000 people. Zero dedicated fitness apps. One team that built the solution.
>
> Thank you."

**Tip:** Pause before **"Thank you."** Make eye contact with the judges. Smile. You're done — own the moment. Don't look down, don't fidget. Just hold the room.

## Likely Q&A Questions

**"How do you get businesses to list on Atlas?"**
> Start with free listings — zero barrier to entry. We reach out directly to gym owners and sports facilities. Once we have user traffic, the value of being listed becomes obvious. Premium upgrades sell themselves.

**"What stops Google Maps from just copying this?"**
> Google Maps is a global platform optimized for general discovery. They won't build hyper-local, fitness-specific features for Cebu. We move faster, we know the local market, and our community data is a moat they can't replicate overnight.

**"How do you verify crowd data if users report it?"**
> Crowd levels are community-reported, similar to Waze traffic reports. We use aggregation — multiple check-ins in a short window create a reliable signal. Over time, our prediction engine uses historical patterns so even without live check-ins, we can estimate busyness accurately.

**"Why Cebu specifically?"**
> Cebu is the second-largest urban center in the Philippines, with a fast-growing fitness culture and a large BPO workforce that actively seeks fitness options near their offices. It's the perfect proving ground before expanding nationally.

**"What's your user acquisition strategy?"**
> Three channels: organic social (TikTok and Instagram fitness content), gym partnerships where we co-promote with listed businesses, and word of mouth from the fitness community. The product itself is the marketing — when someone finds a great spot through Atlas, they tell their gym friends.
